My Story

My name is Saman, and I am a Kurdish guy from Iraq. I was born on February 12, 2002, in the Kurdistan region of Iraq. From a young age, I learned what it meant to be Kurdish and the struggles my people have faced throughout history.

I started my education at the Classical School of the Medes and graduated in the summer of 2020. At that time, I was unsure about my future. Coming from a family with four doctors, I decided not to pursue medicine because I am naturally anxious and easily stressed—qualities that I felt were not suitable for a doctor.

Choosing a major was difficult for me, as I never found anything particularly enjoyable. Eventually, I chose computer science, mainly because it allowed me to avoid social interactions as much as possible due to my social anxiety. However, studying in Iraq was not an option for me due to economic and political reasons. I needed a country that matched my financial situation, so I chose Hungary.

I moved to Hungary on August 21, 2021, and started my foundation year—a new beginning in a completely different environment. Adapting to the country took time, as I met people with very different cultures, personalities, and senses of humor. Some of those people have stayed with me throughout my journey, and I appreciate their presence in my life. Other than my university coursemates, who have been with me for quite some time, I have only one Hungarian friend after four years in the country, but he is a great person and someone I truly appreciate.

In 2022, I began studying Computer Engineering. My first two semesters were very difficult—I did not like the university system at all, and I even failed one of the most important subjects in my first year. Now, I am in my sixth semester and will need to add an extra semester. My experience as a third-year student has not been great, and I have come to believe that university is more of a "ticket" for companies to trust that you have knowledge rather than a place of real learning. I will graduate next year, but I feel uncomfortable calling myself a computer engineer. Maybe this is the reality everywhere, but for now, I do not see myself applying for jobs anytime soon.

I am now 23 years old, and I still struggle to find something I truly enjoy. People have suggested different activities—sports, music, socializing, even partying—but none of them really help. I do like going out with my friends from time to time, but I am still searching for something that makes me feel mentally comfortable.

Right now, besides studying, I spend my time gaming and watching anime. Let’s see what happens after graduation.
